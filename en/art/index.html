Play Art Now 🥭

Hangmango's Art category spans techniques, materials, movements and concepts from the history of art. From everyday creative vocabulary to advanced art historical terms, this category is ideal for art students and enthusiasts.

How to play the Art category

Art vocabulary draws from French, Italian and German origins — many terms are borrowed directly from European art history. Look for French-origin words in medium difficulty (aquarelle, lithography, pastel). Hard difficulty features major art movements that follow a predictable "-ism" pattern — impressionism, cubism, surrealism — which can help you guess the ending once you identify the movement.

Sample art words

  • Easy: painting, drawing, sculpture, photo, dance, theatre, cinema, novel, poem, fresco, portrait, colour, brush, canvas
  • Medium: watercolour, engraving, mosaic, calligraphy, ceramics, lithography, pastel, stained glass, tapestry, collage, charcoal, animation, typography, etching
  • Hard: impressionism, surrealism, renaissance, baroque, romanticism, cubism, expressionism, naturalism, symbolism, classicism, dadaism, futurism, realism, modernism, bauhaus

Educational value

Art history vocabulary is central to GCSE and A-Level Art and Design curricula. The hard difficulty words map directly to the art movements students are expected to know and discuss analytically.
